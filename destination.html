Famous Places in Mangalore

St. Aloysius Chapel

This 19th-century chapel is famous for its Italian frescoes by Antonio Moscheni and is one of Mangalore's most visited heritage sites.

Mangaladevi Temple

An ancient temple after which the city is named. Built by the Alupa dynasty, attracts thousands of devotees especially during Dasara.

Kudroli Gokarnanath Temple

A Dravidian-style temple built by Sri Narayana Guru. A major pilgrimage site with a towering golden gopuram.

Kadri Manjunatha Temple

Dating back to the 10th century, this temple sits on Kadri Hill surrounded by sacred ponds and greenery.

Milagres Church

Built in 1680 and reconstructed later. A key Catholic landmark with deep historical significance.

Sultan Battery

A watchtower built by Tipu Sultan in the 18th century, offering beautiful coastal views.

Pilikula Nisargadhama

An eco-tourism park with a zoo, lake, botanical garden & India's first 8K 3D planetarium.

Srimanthi Bai Government Museum

Established in 1865. Displays coins, manuscripts, sculptures, and historical artifacts.

Adyar Waterfalls

Adyar Waterfalls, located about 15 kilometers from Mangalore, is a serene and picturesque destination, perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Mangalore Central Market

A bustling local market famous for spices, flowers, vegetables, and coastal products.
